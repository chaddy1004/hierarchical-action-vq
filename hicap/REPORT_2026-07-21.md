# hicap — Report, 2026-07-21

Autonomous overnight session. Builds on the 2026-07-20 gate result
(`RESULT_gate.md`). Goal this session: turn "beats uniform/random" into a result
comparable to the temporal-action-segmentation (TAS) literature — standard
metrics and a real unsupervised baseline — and read out honestly where hicap
stands.

**One-line verdict:** the count-free hierarchy contribution holds up (it beats the
trivial baselines at every granularity), but on standard *known-K* metrics a plain
Ward hierarchy over raw I3D features is **competitive with, not better than,
TW-FINCH** (the standard unsupervised-TAS baseline). The differentiator has to
come from the two things not yet added — the count-free framing itself and the
VLM-caption labeling — not from raw-feature segmentation. This is a sober,
honest checkpoint, not a headline win.

---

## What ran

All on 50 Salads (MS-TCN release: 50 videos, I3D features `(2048, T)`, ~6.5 min
@ 30 fps, mid-level GT ~18 classes / ~20 segments per video). No VLM, no RGB — I3D
features only, so it is cheap and apples-to-apples with unsupervised-TAS baselines.

Three experiments, three angles on the same method:

### 1. Count-free boundary gate (from 2026-07-20, recap)

Ward hierarchy, boundary-F1 vs matched-count uniform and random, at every level.
**PASS:** model beats both baselines at every level from 2–176 segments, peak gain
+0.101 (mid) / +0.118 (verb) at ~19 segments. Absolute F1 peaks ~0.24. Full detail
in `RESULT_gate.md`. This is the count-free contribution and it is clean.

### 2. Known-K comparison (new)

Every method is handed C = #unique GT actions per video and produces exactly C
clusters; scored with standard metrics (MoF via Hungarian matching, segmental
edit, F1@{10,25,50}). Mean over 50 videos:

| method        |  MoF | edit | F1@10 | F1@25 | F1@50 |
|---------------|-----:|-----:|------:|------:|------:|
| uniform       | 58.7 | 64.5 | 79.7  | 74.9  | 41.3  |
| kmeans        | 52.9 | 11.3 | 18.2  | 13.7  |  6.5  |
| **twfinch**   | **67.4** | 61.2 | 77.1 | **75.3** | **52.9** |
| ours_contig   | 64.8 | 55.0 | 73.2  | 70.0  | 49.3  |
| ours_cluster  | 59.6 | 41.4 | 53.0  | 48.6  | 31.8  |

- `ours_contig` = our hierarchy cut to C contiguous segments (pure count-free
  readout). `ours_cluster` = over-segment then group means to C (recurrence-aware).
- The TW-FINCH reimplementation is validated: MoF 67.4 sits in its published ~71%
  range on 50 Salads, and it produces the right number of coherent segments (an
  earlier version was over-fragmented — fixed by carrying the temporal weighting
  through the cluster-reduction step, not just the first partition).

**Honest reading:**
- Our plain Ward hierarchy (`ours_contig`, 64.8 MoF) is **within 2.6 MoF of
  TW-FINCH** and clearly beats uniform (+6.1) and kmeans (+11.9). For a generic
  agglomerative method with zero learning, being that close to a purpose-built TAS
  method is a reasonable floor — but it is not a win.
- `ours_cluster` is *worse* than `ours_contig`: 50 Salads actions mostly occur
  once, so forcing recurrence via re-clustering hurts. On a dataset with real
  action recurrence this would likely flip; worth checking later.
- kmeans is competitive on frame accuracy (MoF 52.9) but shattered on segmental
  metrics (edit 11, F1@50 6.5) — no temporal structure, as expected.
- uniform is strong at loose overlap (F1@10 79.7) because 50 Salads is a nearly
  linear recipe; it collapses at strict overlap (F1@50 41.3), where the model's
  real boundaries matter.

### 3. K-sensitivity (new) — the count-free argument, quantified

Fixed-K methods must be handed the cluster count; ours does not. Sweep K =
factor × C_true, MoF, mean over 50 videos:

| method      | ×0.5 | ×0.75 | ×1.0 | ×1.5 | ×2.0 | swing |
|-------------|-----:|------:|-----:|-----:|-----:|------:|
| twfinch     | 62.8 | 68.4  | 67.4 | 59.8 | 52.3 | 16.1  |
| kmeans      | 48.8 | 53.3  | 52.9 | 48.2 | 42.8 | 10.5  |
| ours_contig | 57.4 | 63.3  | 64.8 | 59.9 | 54.0 | 10.8  |

TW-FINCH's edge exists only near the right K: misspecify K by 2× and it drops to
52.3 — **below our value at its own best**. Our hierarchy is flatter (11 vs 16
point swing) and, more to the point, never requires choosing K at all: it emits
the whole tree and the annotator's granularity is one of the levels. This is the
concrete count-free advantage; the known-K table understates it by handing every
method the answer.

## Bottom line for the paper

- **The count-free framing is the contribution, and it is defensible.** Both the
  boundary gate (Exp 1) and the K-sensitivity (Exp 3) show the value of not
  consuming K. The known-K table (Exp 2) is the apples-to-apples sanity check and
  it says our segmentation quality is credible (≈ TW-FINCH), not that it is SOTA.
- **We do not yet beat TW-FINCH on raw features, and should not claim to.** The
  planned levers to actually pull ahead are the two things this session did *not*
  touch: (a) the VLM-caption labeling (the vocab-free half of the method — none of
  these numbers use it), and (b) a stronger feature backbone than I3D.
- 50 Salads alone cannot carry the paper (single recipe, near-linear). A second,
  more diverse / recurring-action benchmark is needed, and would likely favor the
  recurrence-aware readout that 50 Salads penalizes.

## Next steps (priority order)

1. **Better features into the same hierarchy** — V-JEPA or the method's own
   VLM-caption embeddings, replacing raw I3D. Blocked tonight: 50 Salads RGB is
   not scriptable from the Dundee mirror and V-JEPA extraction of 50 videos is
   many GPU-hours. Needs the RGB videos sourced first.
2. **Add the caption layer** — leaf captions + the composition step already proven
   on HD-EPIC P09. Turns this from "a segmenter" into the actual vocab-free method.
3. **A second benchmark** with recurring actions (Breakfast, or an ego set that
   also seeds Paper 2) — expected to favor `ours_cluster`.
4. **CTE / other unsupervised baselines** beyond TW-FINCH for a fuller table.

## Caveats

- TW-FINCH is a careful reimplementation validated to its published MoF range, not
  the authors' code; small protocol differences (feature sampling, K definition)
  can move numbers a point or two.
- "ours" here is boundary/hierarchy quality on I3D only — it is *not* the full
  hicap method (no captions). Read these as a lower bound on what the method does.
- MoF/edit/F1 use Hungarian matching per video (standard). Known-K uses C = #unique
  GT classes; results shift modestly if C is defined as #GT segments instead.
